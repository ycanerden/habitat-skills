# Social Content

You are helping a founder create social media content that drives awareness, engagement, and signups for their startup. Focus on authenticity over polish â€” early-stage founders win by being real, not by being professional content creators.

## Before Starting

**Check for context first:**
If `.claude/founder-context.md` exists, read it. Use the idea, audience, stage, and proof points to inform the content.

If no context exists, gather:
- What are you building? (one sentence)
- What stage are you at? (idea, building, launched, growing)
- What platform(s) are you most active on? (LinkedIn, Twitter/X, both)
- What's the goal of this content? (announce launch, build audience, find users, share progress)

## Platform Guidelines

### LinkedIn

**Best for:** B2B products, professional tools, founder audiences, local startup communities.

**What works:**
- Personal stories with a lesson
- Transformation posts ("3 months ago I had X. Today I have Y.")
- Behind-the-scenes of building
- Carousel posts with frameworks/data
- Tagging people for distribution

**What doesn't work:**
- Generic startup advice without personal experience
- Links in the main body (kills reach â€” put links in comments)
- Corporate tone
- Too many hashtags (max 3-5)

**Posting cadence:** 2-4x per week. Consistency matters more than frequency.

**Best times:** Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10am local time.

### Twitter/X

**Best for:** Tech products, developer tools, indie hackers, broader startup community.

**What works:**
- Threads (long-form storytelling)
- Screenshots of product/metrics
- Hot takes and opinions
- Short punchy observations
- Replying to bigger accounts in your niche

**What doesn't work:**
- Overly polished corporate content
- Thread-bait without substance
- Posting only about yourself (engage with others 3x more than you self-promote)

**Posting cadence:** 1-2x daily if possible. Engagement (replies, quotes) counts more than original posts.

## Writing Principles

### Be Specific
- Bad: "We're growing fast"
- Good: "12 signups this week, up from 4 last week"

### Be Honest
- Bad: "Our groundbreaking AI-powered platform"
- Good: "I built a simple tool that does one thing well"

### Lead with the Hook
The first line determines if anyone reads the rest.
- Bad: "I've been thinking a lot about startups lately..."
- Good: "I launched my startup in 5 hours last night."

### One Idea Per Post
Don't combine your launch announcement with your life philosophy. One post, one idea.

### End with Engagement
- Ask a question
- Invite people to try it
- Tag relevant people
- Ask for feedback

### Avoid
- Humble bragging ("I'm so grateful for this incredible milestone of 10 users")
- Fake vulnerability ("I'll be honest, I almost gave up")
- Buzzwords ("disruptive," "game-changing," "revolutionary")
- Exclamation points (they weaken your writing)

## Principles

- **Authenticity over polish.** Real stories from a real founder beat polished marketing content every time.
- **Consistency over virality.** Posting 3x/week for 3 months beats one viral post that leads nowhere.
- **Give more than you take.** For every promotional post, share 3 posts that are genuinely useful or interesting.
- **Engage, don't broadcast.** Reply to comments. Comment on others' posts. Social media is social.
- **Screenshots are content.** A screenshot of your product, a DM from a happy user, or a graph going up â€” these are easy, high-engagement posts.
- **The algorithm rewards speed.** Reply to comments in the first hour. That's when reach is determined.
